# Case Type Analysis: Litigation Finance Investment

Litigation finance has matured into a sophisticated asset class, offering non-correlated returns for institutional investors. However, the risk-return profile, duration, and market dynamics vary significantly across case types. Below is a comprehensive analysis of the eight primary case types funded in the market today.

---

## 1. Commercial Litigation
**Examples:** Breach of contract, fraud, IP disputes (trade secret, copyright/trademark)
Commercial litigation is the bedrock of the litigation finance market. These are typically binary disputes between two corporate entities where the plaintiff seeks compensatory or punitive damages. Funder due diligence focuses heavily on the defendant's ability to pay (collectability) and the strength of the legal merits.

*   **Typical Duration:** 2 to 4 years.
*   **Expected Returns:** 2.0x – 4.0x MOIC (Multiple on Invested Capital), or 20%–40% IRR.
*   **Risk Profile:** **Moderate to High.** The binary risk of summary judgment or trial loss is significant. However, fraud and breach of contract cases often have clear paper trails, which can mitigate merits risk. The primary risk is often collection risk if the defendant becomes insolvent.
*   **Current Market Rates:** 2.5x–4.0x multiples on invested capital are standard for single-case funding. Portfolios of commercial cases see lower rates (1.5x–2.5x) in exchange for diversified risk.

## 2. Patent Monetization
**Examples:** Patent assertion, PTAB (Patent Trial and Appeal Board) challenges, portfolio funding
Patent monetization involves funding a patent holder (often an NPE or individual inventor) against an infringer. This is highly specialized due to the technical complexity and the unique procedural hurdles of patent law, particularly the Inter Partes Review (IPR) process at the PTAB, which can kill a patent before it reaches trial.

*   **Typical Duration:** 3 to 5+ years (highly variable due to PTAB stays and appeals).
*   **Expected Returns:** 3.0x – 6.0x MOIC (higher end for single patents, lower for large portfolio funding).
*   **Risk Profile:** **High.** Invalidity risk at the PTAB is substantial. Even if the patent survives, damages apportionment can severely reduce the payout. However, portfolio funding (funding 10+ patents against multiple defendants) mitigates this through diversification.
*   **Current Market Rates:** Due to the elevated invalidity risk and longer duration, patent assertion demands a premium. Rates typically range from 3.0x–5.0x for single assertions, while PTAB defense funding is often priced at lower multiples (1.5x–2.5x) due to shorter duration.

## 3. International Arbitration
**Examples:** BIT (Bilateral Investment Treaty) claims, ICC arbitration, cross-border commercial disputes
International arbitration involves disputes that cross jurisdictions, often involving sovereign states (BIT claims) or multinational corporations. Awards are enforceable under the New York Convention, but enforcement against sovereign assets presents unique challenges.

*   **Typical Duration:** 3 to 6 years (BIT claims against states often drag on the longest).
*   **Expected Returns:** 2.5x – 4.0x MOIC.
*   **Risk Profile:** **Moderate.** The legal process is generally predictable, and summary disposition is rare, meaning cases usually reach a hearing. The primary risk is **sovereign immunity and enforcement**—winning a BIT claim against a state is different from actually extracting payment from its central bank.
*   **Current Market Rates:** 2.5x–4.0x for commercial arbitration; BIT claims may see rates of 3.0x–5.0x to compensate for the complex enforcement risk and political volatility.

## 4. Class Actions
**Examples:** Securities, antitrust, consumer protection
Class actions allow funders to finance the representation of a large group of plaintiffs. These cases are often contingency-fee driven by law firms, with funders providing capital for hard costs (expert witnesses, discovery) or serving as a liquidity facility for the lead plaintiffs.

*   **Typical Duration:** 3 to 5 years.
*   **Expected Returns:** 2.0x – 4.0x MOIC.
*   **Risk Profile:** **Moderate.** Class actions carry high procedural risk (certification battles, motions to dismiss). However, once a class is certified, defendants frequently settle, reducing the binary trial risk. Antitrust and securities cases often feature deep-pocketed defendants, ensuring collectability.
*   **Current Market Rates:** 2.0x–3.5x
