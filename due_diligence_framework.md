# Due Diligence Framework for Litigation Finance Investments

This framework provides a comprehensive methodology for evaluating, structuring, and managing litigation finance investments. Given the unique, binary, and illiquid nature of legal assets, a rigorous, multi-disciplinary approach bridging legal analysis and financial modeling is required.

---

## 1. Case Evaluation Criteria

The foundational layer of due diligence focuses on the intrinsic legal and financial viability of the individual claim.

| Criterion | Description | Assessment Methodology |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Merit Score** | The objective probability of the case succeeding on the merits. | Independent legal review; analysis of evidence, applicable law, and precedent; discounting for procedural vulnerabilities. |
| **Damages Potential** | The ceiling of recoverable damages relative to investment. | Review of expert economic reports; categorization of damages (compensatory, punitive, disgorgement); assessment of trebling potential in statutory claims. |
| **Defendant Solvency** | The ability of the defendant to pay a judgment or settlement. | Credit analysis; review of financial statements, asset registers, and encumbrances; identification of insurance coverage and indemnity agreements. |

---

## 2. Legal Team Assessment

In litigation finance, the legal team is the operational management of the asset. Their competence directly dictates the probability and timing of returns.

*   **Track Record:** Historical win rates and settlement ratios in comparable cases. Experience taking cases through trial and appeal, rather than just settling favorably.
*   **Specialization:** Deep subject matter expertise in the specific legal area (e.g., patent infringement, antitrust, international arbitration). Generalists are viewed with a discount.
*   **Firm Reputation:** The institutional weight of the firm. A premier firm signals credibility to defendants and courts, often forcing earlier and higher settlements. Assessment of firm stability and retention of key personnel.

---

## 3. Jurisdiction Analysis

The venue can be as important as the facts of the case. Jurisdictional dynamics heavily influence duration, costs, and outcomes.

*   **Court Efficiency:** Analysis of docket speed, backlog, and time-to-trial metrics (e.g., ED Texas vs. SDNY).
*   **Judge Assignment:** Review of the assigned judge’s historical tendencies regarding claim construction, summary judgment grants, and trial management.
*   **Jury Tendencies:** Demographic analysis of the jury pool; historical propensity for plaintiff vs. defense verdicts; historical award ranges in the specific venue.

---

## 4. Portfolio Construction

To mitigate the binary risk inherent in individual cases, a portfolio approach must be implemented to smooth returns and reduce variance.

| Diversification Axis | Strategy | Rationale |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Case Type** | Blend patent, antitrust, commercial, and international arbitration. | Prevents sector-wide regulatory or precedent shifts from wiping out the portfolio. |
| **Geography** | Spread across domestic federal/state courts and international arbitral forums (ICSID, LCIA). | Mitigates jurisdiction-specific legal reforms or systemic court closures. |
| **Stage** | Mix pre-filing, post-discovery, summary judgment, and appellate cases. | Balances the high-IRR/long-duration of early cases with the low-IRR/short-duration of late-stage cases to manage cash flows. |

---

## 5. Risk Scoring Matrix

A quantitative model is required to objectively compare disparate cases. The following matrix calculates a composite Risk-Adjusted Score (RAS) to dictate case selection.

| Risk Factor | Weight | Score (1-5) | Description of Score 1 (Lowest Risk) | Description of Score 5 (Highest Risk) |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Legal Merit** | 30% | ___ | Clear precedent, overwhelming evidence | Novel claim, adverse precedent |
| **Damages Certainty** | 20% | ___ | Liquidated damages, contractually fixed | Speculative lost profits, untested punis |
| **Solvency Risk** | 20% | ___ | Investment-grade counterparty, insurance | Distressed entity, offshore shell co. |
| **Duration Risk** | 15% | ___ | Post-discovery, fixed trial date | Pre-filing, complex discovery anticipated |
| **Adverse Party Risk**| 15% | ___ | Passive defendant, motivated to settle | Vexatious litigant, infinite war chest |

*Calculation:* (Weight × Score) for each row. Target investments must achieve a RAS below a defined threshold (e.g., ≤ 2.5).
