# Investor Qualification & Onboarding Guide for Litigation Finance

This guide outlines the regulatory, financial, and administrative requirements for qualifying and onboarding investors into litigation finance funds and direct case investments. Litigation finance is a specialized, illiquid asset class requiring rigorous compliance and thorough investor understanding.

---

## 1. Accredited Investor Requirements

To invest in private litigation finance offerings under Regulation D, investors must first meet the Accredited Investor criteria defined by the SEC.

### Income Thresholds
- [ ] **Individual Income:** Earned over $200,000 in each of the two most recent years, with a reasonable expectation of the same for the current year.
- [ ] **Joint Income:** Earned over $300,000 with a spouse/partner in each of the two most recent years, with a reasonable expectation of the same for the current year.

### Net Worth Thresholds
- [ ] **Individual or Joint Net Worth:** Exceeds $1,000,000, either alone or together with a spouse/partner.
- [ ] **Exclusion of Primary Residence:** The net worth calculation *must exclude* the investor's primary residence. (Note: Indebtedness secured by the residence is only excluded up to the fair market value of the residence; excess debt reduces net worth).

### Professional Credentials
- [ ] **Certifications:** Hold certain professional certifications or designations (e.g., Series 7, 65, 82) in good standing, or qualify as a "Knowledgeable Employee" of the fund.

## 2. Qualified Purchaser Status

For funds structured under Section 3(c)(7) of the Investment Company Act of 1940 (allowing more than 100 investors and higher fee structures), investors must qualify as a Qualified Purchaser (QP).

### QP Thresholds (Must meet at least one)
- [ ] **Individual:** Owns $5,000,000+ in "Investable Assets" (securities, real estate held for investment, cash, etc.).
- [ ] **Family Company:** Owns $5,000,000+ in investable assets, owned directly or indirectly by family members.
- [ ] **Trust:** Not formed for the specific purpose of acquiring fund interests, with $5,000,000+ in investable assets, directed by a sophisticated trustee.
- [ ] **Entity:** Owns $25,000,000+ in investable assets (acting for own account or accounts of other QPs).
- [ ] **QP-Owned Entity:** An entity where 100% of the equity owners are Qualified Purchasers.

## 3. KYC/AML Requirements

Litigation finance funds are prime targets for money laundering due to the opaque nature of legal systems and cross-border capital flows. Strict Know Your Customer (KYC) and Anti-Money Laundering (AML) compliance is mandatory.

### Identity Verification
- [ ] **Individuals:** Government-issued photo ID (Passport, Driver’s License) and proof of address (utility bill, bank statement dated within 60 days).
- [ ] **Entities:** Certified Articles of Incorporation/Organization, Operating Agreement, Bylaws, and Certificate of Good Standing.
- [ ] **Beneficial Ownership:** Identification of all individuals with 25%+ equity interest, and a single controlling person (for entities).

### Source of Funds (SOF) & Source of Wealth (SOW)
- [ ] **SOW Verification:** Documentation proving how the investor accumulated their overall wealth (e.g., sale of business, inheritance, employment history).
- [ ] **SOF Verification:** Documentation proving the origin of the specific capital being invested (e.g., recent bank statements, liquidity event docs, line of credit statements).
- [ ] **Enhanced Due Diligence (EDD):** Required for Politically Exposed Persons (PEPs), investors from high-risk jurisdictions, or complex multi-layered trust structures.

## 4. Risk Disclosure: Mandatory Warnings

Litigation finance is a non-recourse, highly idiosyncratic asset class. Investors must acknowledge the following mandatory risk disclosures:

- [ ] **Total Loss of Capital:** The investment is non-recourse. If the funded case is lost or settled unfavorably, the investor is entitled to nothing and loses 100% of their deployed capital.
- [ ] **Illiquidity:** There is no secondary market for litigation finance interests. Investors cannot sell or transfer their interests without fund manager approval.
- [ ] **Binary Outcomes:** Returns are highly dependent on court verdicts, settlements, or arbitration awards, which are inherently unpredictable.
